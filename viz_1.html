Max Temperature

Latitude refers to the distance of a location on Earth's surface from the equator. Latitude effects temperature due to the angle of incidence(the angle at which the sun's rays strike Earth's surface). Regions closer to Earth’s equator experience higher temperatures due to the sun being positioned directly above Earth's surface therefore the sun’s rays strike directly resulting in higher temperatures. If the angle of incidence was larger such as with regions that are located further from the equator, the sun's rays would spread out over a larger surface area with less intensity, making these regions experience lower temperatures.
